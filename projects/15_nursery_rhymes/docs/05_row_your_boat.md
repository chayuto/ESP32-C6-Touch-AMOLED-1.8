# Row Row Row Your Boat

## Origin
American nursery rhyme first published in 1852, with the melody attributed to Eliphalet Oram Lyte (1860). One of the most well-known rounds (canons) in English. Often one of the first rounds children learn to sing.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 6/8
- **Tempo:** ~100 BPM (dotted quarter = beat)
- **Range:** C4–C5 (octave)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics
Row, row, row your boat,
Gently down the stream.
Merrily, merrily, merrily, merrily,
Life is but a dream.

## Analysis
A classic round/canon — can be sung in 2-4 parts entering sequentially. The "merrily" section features rapid repeated notes stepping down from C5 through the C major triad (C-G-E-C). The 6/8 meter gives it a gentle rocking feel appropriate to the rowing imagery.
