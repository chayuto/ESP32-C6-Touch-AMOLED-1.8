# Mary Had a Little Lamb

## Origin
Written by Sarah Josepha Hale, published 1830. Based on a true story of Mary Sawyer of Sterling, Massachusetts, whose pet lamb followed her to school. Thomas Edison recorded this rhyme on the first phonograph in 1877.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4–G4 (perfect 5th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics
Mary had a little lamb,
Little lamb, little lamb,
Mary had a little lamb,
Its fleece was white as snow.

And everywhere that Mary went,
Mary went, Mary went,
Everywhere that Mary went,
The lamb was sure to go.

## Analysis
Very narrow range (just 5 notes: C-D-E-F-G). Descending third (E-D-C) is the signature motif. One of the easiest melodies to play, making it ideal for young children.
