# If You're Happy and You Know It

## Origin
Action song likely derived from a Latvian folk song, popularized in English by the American folklorist and children's entertainer Dr. Alfred B. Smith in the 1950s-1960s. The call-and-response format with clapping/stomping makes it highly interactive.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4-G4 (perfect 5th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics (Verse 1)
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands.
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands.
If you're happy and you know it, and you really want to show it,
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands.

## Analysis
The rests after "clap your hands" are where the clapping action occurs — a brilliant integration of music and movement. The melody sits in a comfortable range. The bridge ("and you really want to show it") provides harmonic variety before returning to the main phrase. Subsequent verses substitute different actions (stomp your feet, shout hooray, do all three).
