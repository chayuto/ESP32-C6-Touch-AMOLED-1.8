# Hush Little Baby (Mockingbird)

## Origin
Traditional American lullaby, origins uncertain but likely 18th-19th century Southern United States. The cumulative structure (each verse introduces a new gift if the previous one fails) makes it endlessly extendable. Public domain.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~100 BPM
- **Range:** C4-A4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Note Sequence
```
C4(e) C4(e) E4 G4 | G4 A4(e) G4(e) |
E4 E4 D4 E4 | F4 E4 D4 - |
C4(e) C4(e) E4 G4 | G4 A4(e) G4(e) |
E4 E4 D4 E4 | F4 E4 C4 - |
```

## Lyrics (First Two Verses)
Hush, little baby, don't say a word,
Mama's gonna buy you a mockingbird.
And if that mockingbird don't sing,
Mama's gonna buy you a diamond ring.

## Analysis
Gentle lullaby with a narrow range and repetitive melodic pattern. The two-phrase structure repeats with only the final cadence changing (D4 vs C4). The pickup rhythm (two eighths) gives a rocking feel. Cumulative lyric structure lets parents improvise additional verses.
