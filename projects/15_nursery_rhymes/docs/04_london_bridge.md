# London Bridge Is Falling Down

## Origin
One of the best-known English nursery rhymes. Earliest printed version dates to 1744 in "Tommy Thumb's Pretty Song Book." Origins debated — theories link it to Viking attacks on London Bridge (1014), or it may simply describe the bridge's frequent disrepair.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** D4–A4 (perfect 5th)

## Analysis
Characteristic dotted-quarter + eighth rhythm on the opening "London Bridge is." The melody outlines a simple arch shape, rising then falling. The final "my fair lady" cadence drops to the tonic C.
