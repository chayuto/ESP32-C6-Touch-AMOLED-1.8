# Jack and Jill

## Origin
Traditional English nursery rhyme first published in 1765 in John Newbery's "Mother Goose's Melody." Some historians link it to the arrest of Cardinal Wolsey (Jack) or the French Revolution's Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette. Most likely it's simply a children's cautionary tale.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 6/8
- **Tempo:** ~108 BPM
- **Range:** C4–A4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics (Verse 1)
Jack and Jill went up the hill,
To fetch a pail of water.
Jack fell down and broke his crown,
And Jill came tumbling after.

## Lyrics (Verse 2)
Up Jack got, and home did trot,
As fast as he could caper,
He went to bed to mend his head,
With vinegar and brown paper.

## Analysis
A stepwise ascending melody mirrors "going up the hill." The 6/8 compound meter gives it a skipping, jaunty feel. The descending phrase at "tumbling after" provides natural word painting.
