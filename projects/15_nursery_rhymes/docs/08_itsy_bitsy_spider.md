# Itsy Bitsy Spider

## Origin
American nursery rhyme and fingerplay song, first published as "Blousicle Spider" in 1910 in "Camp and Camino in Lower California." Also known as "Incy Wincy Spider" in British English. The accompanying hand gestures make it a classic action song for toddlers.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 6/8
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** G4–G5 (octave)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate

## Lyrics
The itsy bitsy spider climbed up the water spout.
Down came the rain, and washed the spider out.
Out came the sun, and dried up all the rain,
And the itsy bitsy spider climbed up the spout again.

## Analysis
Higher register (G4-G5) than most nursery rhymes. The ascending pattern on "climbed up" and descending on "washed the spider out" provide excellent word painting. The 6/8 meter creates a lilting, playful character. The melody returns to the opening for the final "climbed up the spout again."
