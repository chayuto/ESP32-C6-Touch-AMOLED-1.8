# Frere Jacques (Are You Sleeping / Brother John)

## Origin
Traditional French nursery rhyme, likely dating to the 17th century. Widely attributed to Jean-Philippe Rameau but this is unconfirmed. One of the most widely known rounds in the world, translated into dozens of languages. In English: "Are You Sleeping, Brother John?"

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4–G4 (perfect 5th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Analysis
A perfect 4-part round — each phrase is exactly one measure. The melody is entirely stepwise within C major scale. The "sonnez les matines" section introduces the only ornamental eighth-note figures. The "din din don" cadence (C-G3-C) provides a bell-like bass resolution using the widest interval in the song.
