# Wheels on the Bus

## Origin
American children's song written by Verna Hills in 1939. Published in "American Childhood" magazine. An action song where children mime the movements described (wheels turning, wipers swishing, horn beeping). One of the most popular preschool songs worldwide.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4-G4 (perfect 5th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics (Verse 1)
The wheels on the bus go round and round,
Round and round, round and round.
The wheels on the bus go round and round,
All through the town.

## Analysis
Very simple melody using only 5 notes (C-D-E-F-G). The repetitive structure supports the action-song format where each verse substitutes a new bus element (wipers, horn, doors, babies, etc.). The ascending C-E-G arpeggio gives it a bright, cheerful character.
