# Old MacDonald Had a Farm

## Origin
One of the most popular children's songs worldwide. Earliest known version is "The Farmyard" from Thomas d'Urfey's opera "The Kingdom of the Birds" (1706). The modern "Old MacDonald" form was published in 1917. The call-and-response structure with animal sounds makes it interactive.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4–A4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Lyrics
Old MacDonald had a farm, E-I-E-I-O.
And on his farm he had a cow, E-I-E-I-O.
With a moo-moo here and a moo-moo there,
Here a moo, there a moo, everywhere a moo-moo.
Old MacDonald had a farm, E-I-E-I-O.

## Analysis
Same melody as "Twinkle" for the verse sections. The "E-I-E-I-O" refrain (F-F-E-E-D) provides structure. The animal sound section is rhythmically free and interactive — children can substitute any animal. The repeated C4 notes in the "here a moo" section create a chanting effect.
