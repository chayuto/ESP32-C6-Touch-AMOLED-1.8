# Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes

## Origin
Action song of unknown authorship, widely popular since at least the mid-20th century. Children touch each body part as it's named, with the song getting progressively faster. Used worldwide in preschool education for body awareness and coordination.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM (often accelerates each repeat)
- **Range:** C4-A4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner

## Note Sequence
```
C4 E4 G4 G4 | G4 E4 G4 - |
C4 E4 G4 G4 | G4 E4 G4 - |
A4 A4 A4 G4 | F4 F4 F4 E4 |
D4 E4 F4 D4 | E4 F4 C4 - - - |
```

## Lyrics
Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes.
Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes.
And eyes, and ears, and mouth, and nose.
Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes.

## Analysis
The ascending C major arpeggio (C-E-G) maps perfectly to the descending body parts (head=high, toes=low... well, musically ascending but bodily descending). The bridge section ("eyes and ears") introduces stepwise descending motion. The accelerating-repeat tradition makes it a physical coordination challenge.
