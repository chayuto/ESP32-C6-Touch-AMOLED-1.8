# Yankee Doodle

## Origin
Anglo-American song with origins in the 1750s-1760s. Originally a British song mocking colonial Americans, it was adopted by American patriots during the Revolutionary War as a song of defiance and pride. The melody may derive from a medieval harvest song.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** G3–E4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate

## Lyrics (Verse 1)
Yankee Doodle went to town,
A-riding on a pony,
Stuck a feather in his hat,
And called it macaroni.

## Lyrics (Chorus)
Yankee Doodle keep it up,
Yankee Doodle dandy,
Mind the music and the step,
And with the girls be handy.

## Analysis
Lower register than most nursery rhymes (centered around G3-E4). The jaunty rhythm with its march-like 4/4 time signature reflects its military origins. The verse uses mostly stepwise motion with a few characteristic leaps (C→G3 on "pony"). The chorus section is more animated.
