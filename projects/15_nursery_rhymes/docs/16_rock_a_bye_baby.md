# Rock-a-Bye Baby

## Origin
One of the first English-language lullabies. First printed in "Mother Goose's Melody" (1765). Possibly written by a Pilgrim passenger on the Mayflower, describing Native American practice of hanging birch-bark cradles in trees. The melody is gentle and waltz-like.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 3/4 (waltz)
- **Tempo:** ~80 BPM (slow lullaby)
- **Range:** D4–D5 (octave)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate

## Lyrics
Rock-a-bye baby, on the treetop,
When the wind blows, the cradle will rock,
When the bough breaks, the cradle will fall,
And down will come baby, cradle and all.

## Analysis
The 3/4 waltz time creates a gentle rocking motion perfect for a lullaby. Each phrase starts with a pickup note pattern (quarter + two eighths) creating a swaying feel. The melody reaches its highest point on "the cradle will fall" (D5) before the dramatic descent on "down will come baby." The slow tempo (80 BPM) makes it soothing for sleep.
