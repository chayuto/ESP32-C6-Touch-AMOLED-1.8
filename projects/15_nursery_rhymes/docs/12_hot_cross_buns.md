# Hot Cross Buns

## Origin
English street cry dating to at least 1733, referencing the Good Friday tradition of selling spiced buns marked with a cross. First published with music in 1798 in "London Street Cries." One of the first songs taught to beginning instrumentalists due to its use of only three notes.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 4/4
- **Tempo:** ~100 BPM
- **Range:** C4–E4 (major 3rd — narrowest range of any common nursery rhyme)
- **Difficulty:** Absolute Beginner

## Lyrics
Hot cross buns! Hot cross buns!
One a penny, two a penny,
Hot cross buns!

## Analysis
Uses only THREE notes (C, D, E) making it the simplest possible melody. The descending third (E-D-C) is the entire harmonic content. The "one a penny" section introduces eighth-note rhythm on repeated pitches. This is universally the first song taught to recorder and piano students.
