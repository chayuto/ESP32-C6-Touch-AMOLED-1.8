# Three Blind Mice

## Origin
One of the oldest English nursery rhymes. A version appears in Thomas Ravenscroft's "Deuteromelia" (1609). The earliest printed version (1609) has a different melody. Often sung as a round. Some historians link the "farmer's wife" to Queen Mary I ("Bloody Mary").

## Lyrics
Three blind mice, three blind mice,
See how they run, see how they run!
They all ran after the farmer's wife,
She cut off their tails with a carving knife.
Did you ever see such a thing in your life,
As three blind mice?

## Analysis
Classic round structure — the opening descending third (E-D-C) is immediately memorable. The repetition of "three blind mice" establishes the pattern. The middle section picks up rhythmic energy with running eighth notes, before resolving back to the opening motif.
