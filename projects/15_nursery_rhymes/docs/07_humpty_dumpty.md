# Humpty Dumpty

## Origin
English nursery rhyme first published in 1797 in Samuel Arnold's "Juvenile Amusements." Originally a riddle (answer: an egg). Famously appears in Lewis Carroll's "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871). Some theories link it to a cannon used during the English Civil War.

## Musical Details
- **Key:** C major
- **Time Signature:** 3/4 (waltz)
- **Tempo:** ~120 BPM
- **Range:** C4–A4 (major 6th)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate

## Lyrics
Humpty Dumpty sat on a wall,
Humpty Dumpty had a great fall.
All the king's horses and all the king's men,
Couldn't put Humpty together again.

## Analysis
The 3/4 waltz time gives it a nursery quality. The E-G-G motif repeats for "Humpty Dumpty" creating a bouncing pattern. The middle section ("All the king's horses") uses rapid quarter notes creating forward momentum before the final descending resolution.
